The Artist

Artist, Designer, and Storyteller

Born in Towson, Maryland, and raised in Winter Park, Florida, Carolyn Jenkins has been painting and creating since childhood. Her artistic journey has taken her from the Maitland Center of the Arts and Rollins College to establishing her own design firm, Storm Hill Studio. Now based in Deltona, she continues to create works in acrylic and watercolor inspired by a lifetime of artistic exploration.

Her commercial career began in the pre-digital era at Tom Griffin Commercial Art Studio in Winter Park, where she specialized in hand-drawn designs for packaging, logos, and brochures. As the industry evolved, she embraced the digital age and built a diverse portfolio including icon art for AOL's early interface, menu design for Walt Disney World and Darden Restaurants, illustrations for the Wayne Taylor Indy Racing team, t-shirt lines for Sea Ray Boats, catalogs for Coach Transit Components, promotional materials for the Orlando Museum of Art, and packaging for brands like Juice Bowl.

In addition to commercial work, Carolyn has spent over fourteen years exhibiting in art festivals across Florida, earning multiple awards for her watercolor paintings. Her work has been featured in gallery exhibits including City Arts Orlando and invitational benefits for Harbor House, and she is an active member of the West Volusia Artists.

Her path as an artist has been defined by both passion and sacrifice. In 1972, she was accepted into the Ringling School of Art, but faced with a family emergency she chose to redirect her college funds to pay for her godmother's life-saving open-heart surgery. That decision gave her godmother twenty more years, and it continues to shape Carolyn's work with gratitude, freedom, and a commitment to creating without judgment.
